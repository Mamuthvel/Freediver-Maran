Straight numbers, no padding. Here is what freediving actually costs in India in 2026, from first breath-hold to fully independent diver.

## Course costs

| Level | Duration | Sea Critter price |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Basic Freediver | 1 day | ₹8,500 |
| PADI Freediver | 2–3 days | ₹18,500 |
| Advanced Freediver | 3–4 days | ₹24,500 |
| Master Freediver | 4–5 days | ₹34,500 |

Prices include eLearning, all equipment, boat fees, and PADI certification. Comparable international pricing: entry certifications run $250–$450 in Southeast Asia and $400–$600 in Europe — India sits at the affordable end of the global range.

## Equipment (optional at first)

All course gear is included, so your real spend can be zero until you're certified and hooked. A complete personal kit — low-volume mask, plastic long-blade fins, snorkel, 3 mm open-cell suit, rubber belt and lead — runs **₹20,000–₹35,000** and lasts years. Full breakdown in our [equipment guide](/blog/freediving-equipment-guide).

## Travel

- **[Pondicherry](/locations/pondicherry):** ₹3,000–₹8,000 for a certification weekend from Chennai or Bengaluru (transport + 2 nights)
- **[Goa](/locations/goa):** flight-dependent; stays from ₹1,500/night
- **[Andamans](/locations/andaman):** ₹15,000–₹25,000 return flights plus ferry; budget ₹40,000–₹60,000 for a week including a course
- **[Maldives](/locations/maldives):** our guesthouse-based trips bring a week to roughly ₹90,000–₹1,40,000 all-in — resort-island pricing not required

## The long game

Here's freediving's quiet superpower: **after certification, the running cost approaches zero.** No tanks, no fills, no compulsory boats. A mask, fins, a buddy, and the sea — for the rest of your life. Few sports amortise this well.
